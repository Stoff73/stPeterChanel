Online Safety

Home/Parents/Online Safety

Keeping children safe online is a shared responsibility between school and home. We work together to educate children about staying safe in the digital world.

Report a Concern

If you are concerned about something your child has seen or experienced online, please contact the school immediately. For urgent concerns, contact CEOP.

Report to CEOP

What We Teach

Online safety is embedded throughout our curriculum. Children learn about:

  • Personal information: Keeping personal details private
  • Stranger danger: Not talking to strangers online
  • Cyberbullying: Recognising and reporting bullying behaviour
  • Screen time: Balancing online and offline activities
  • Trusted adults: Knowing who to tell if something upsets them
  • Age-appropriate content: Understanding why some content is not suitable

Tips for Parents

Talk Openly

Have regular conversations about what your child does online. Create an environment where they feel comfortable telling you if something upsets them.

Use Parental Controls

Set up parental controls on devices and internet connections. Check privacy settings on apps and games your child uses.

Manage Screen Time

Set clear boundaries about when and how long children can use devices. Encourage a balance of online and offline activities.

Stay Involved

Know what apps and games your child uses. Keep devices in shared family spaces rather than bedrooms.

Social Media Age Limits

Most social media platforms have a minimum age of 13. This includes:

  • TikTok - 13 years
  • Instagram - 13 years
  • Snapchat - 13 years
  • Facebook - 13 years
  • WhatsApp - 16 years
  • YouTube (account) - 13 years

We strongly advise that primary school children do not use these platforms.
