History of Our School

Home/ About Us/ History

St Peter Chanel Catholic Primary School has a rich history of serving the Catholic community in Bexley.

Our Patron Saint

Our school is named after St Peter Chanel (1803-1841), a French Catholic priest and Marist missionary. He was martyred on the island of Futuna in the South Pacific and is known as the "Protomartyr of Oceania."

St Peter Chanel was canonized by Pope Pius XII in 1954. His feast day is celebrated on 28th April.

Our School's Journey

St Peter Chanel Catholic Primary School has been serving families in the London Borough of Bexley for many years, providing a Catholic education rooted in Gospel values.

The school has grown and developed over the years, always maintaining its commitment to academic excellence and spiritual development.

St Oscar Romero Catholic Academy Trust

We are now a proud member of the St Oscar Romero Catholic Academy Trust, a multi-academy trust serving Catholic schools across South London and beyond.

Being part of this trust allows us to collaborate with other Catholic schools, share best practices, and benefit from additional resources while maintaining our unique identity and community spirit.

Our Parish

We have strong historical links with St Lawrence's Parish, Sidcup, which continues to support our school community today. Many of our families are parishioners, and we work closely together on sacramental preparation and community events.

Looking Forward

As we continue our journey, we remain committed to our mission to "Inspire, Challenge & Empower so through Christ we make a difference."
