History

Home/Curriculum/History

History helps children understand the world they live in by exploring the past. We develop curious historians who can ask questions and investigate.

Our History Vision

We want children to develop a love of history and a secure understanding of British and world history. Our history teaching:

  • Develops chronological understanding
  • Builds knowledge of significant events, people and places
  • Teaches historical skills - questioning, using sources, making connections
  • Develops understanding of cause and consequence
  • Encourages empathy and different perspectives

Topics We Study

KS1

  • Changes within living memory
  • Significant individuals (e.g. Florence Nightingale)
  • Great Fire of London
  • Local history

Lower KS2

  • Stone Age to Iron Age
  • Ancient Egypt
  • Roman Britain
  • Anglo-Saxons and Vikings

Upper KS2

  • Ancient Greece
  • Maya Civilization
  • Local history study
  • A theme beyond 1066

History Skills

Children develop key historical skills including:

  • Asking and answering historical questions
  • Using primary and secondary sources
  • Understanding chronology and timelines
  • Identifying similarities and differences between periods
  • Understanding cause and effect

Enrichment

We bring history to life through:

  • Educational visits to museums and historical sites
  • Visiting speakers and workshops
  • Handling artefacts and replicas
  • Drama and role play
