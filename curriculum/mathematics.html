Mathematics

Home/Curriculum/Mathematics

We aim to develop confident mathematicians who can reason, problem-solve and apply their skills in a range of contexts.

Our Mathematics Vision

We want all children to develop a deep understanding of mathematics. Our maths teaching develops:

  • Fluency: Rapid and accurate recall of key facts
  • Reasoning: The ability to explain and justify thinking
  • Problem Solving: Applying skills to unfamiliar situations

Mastery Approach

We follow a mastery approach to teaching mathematics. This means:

  • All children work on the same concept at the same time
  • We spend longer on key concepts to ensure deep understanding
  • We use concrete, pictorial and abstract representations
  • Varied practice develops fluency and understanding
  • Struggling children get additional support; advanced children go deeper not faster

Times Tables

Fluent recall of times tables is essential for mathematical success. We expect all children to know their times tables up to 12x12 by the end of Year 4. Children use Times Tables Rock Stars to practise at home and school.

In Year 4, children take the national Multiplication Tables Check.

How You Can Help at Home

  • Practise times tables using Times Tables Rock Stars
  • Play number games and card games
  • Involve children in real-life maths - cooking, shopping, measuring
  • Talk positively about maths - avoid saying "I was never good at maths"
  • Support with homework, but let children think for themselves
