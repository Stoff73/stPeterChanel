PSHE & RSE

Home/Curriculum/PSHE & RSE

Personal, Social, Health and Economic Education (PSHE) and Relationships and Sex Education (RSE) prepare children for life in modern Britain.

Our Approach

As a Catholic school, our PSHE and RSE curriculum is delivered within the context of our Catholic faith. We use the 'Life to the Full' programme, which has been developed specifically for Catholic schools and approved by the Catholic Education Service.

PSHE Topics

Our PSHE curriculum covers three core themes:

Health & Wellbeing

  • Mental health and emotional wellbeing
  • Physical health and fitness
  • Healthy eating
  • Keeping safe
  • First aid awareness

Relationships

  • Families and friendships
  • Respecting self and others
  • Online relationships
  • Being safe
  • Bullying prevention

Living in the Wider World

  • Rights and responsibilities
  • British Values
  • Economic wellbeing
  • Careers education
  • Being a good citizen

Relationships & Sex Education

RSE is taught as part of our PSHE curriculum and is age-appropriate. It covers:

  • Family life and loving relationships
  • Friendships and how to be a good friend
  • Keeping safe (including online safety)
  • Respecting ourselves and others
  • Changes during puberty (Years 5 and 6)

Parent Partnership

We believe parents are the first educators of their children. We:

  • Hold parent information sessions about RSE
  • Share resources and information with parents
  • Welcome questions and discussions
  • Respect parents' role in their children's moral education

Right to Withdraw

Parents have the right to withdraw their children from sex education lessons (but not from relationships education or the science curriculum). If you wish to discuss this, please contact the Headteacher.
