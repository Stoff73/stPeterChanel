English

Home/Curriculum/English

English is at the heart of our curriculum. We aim to develop children who are confident readers, effective writers and articulate speakers.

Our English Vision

We believe that a strong foundation in English is essential for success across the curriculum and in life. Our English teaching aims to:

  • Develop a love of reading and foster lifelong readers
  • Enable children to write clearly and effectively for different purposes
  • Build confident speakers and attentive listeners
  • Develop knowledge of spelling, grammar and punctuation
  • Encourage creativity and expression through language

Reading

Phonics

We use a systematic synthetic phonics programme in EYFS and KS1. Daily phonics lessons develop children's ability to decode and blend sounds.

Guided Reading

Small group reading sessions where children read together and develop comprehension skills through discussion and questioning.

Class Novels

Every class enjoys a class novel read aloud by the teacher, developing vocabulary and a love of literature.

Home Reading

Children take home reading books matched to their phonics level and are encouraged to read daily with parents.

Writing

We teach writing through high-quality texts that inspire and engage children. Our writing curriculum includes:

  • A range of fiction and non-fiction genres
  • Explicit teaching of grammar, punctuation and spelling
  • Handwriting practice and presentation
  • Planning, drafting and editing skills
  • Cross-curricular writing opportunities

Speaking & Listening

Oracy is woven throughout our curriculum. Children develop speaking and listening skills through:

  • Classroom discussions and debates
  • Drama and role play
  • Presentations and performances
  • Talk partners and group work
  • Assemblies and public speaking opportunities

Spelling & Grammar

We teach spelling and grammar explicitly, with weekly spelling sessions and grammar integrated into English lessons. Children use Spelling Shed to practise at home.

How You Can Help at Home

  • Read with your child every day
  • Discuss books and ask questions
  • Encourage your child to write - letters, stories, diaries
  • Play word games and do puzzles
  • Use Spelling Shed for spelling practice
