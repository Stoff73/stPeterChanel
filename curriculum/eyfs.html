Early Years Foundation Stage

Home/Curriculum/EYFS

The Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) covers our Preschool and Reception classes, providing the foundation for children's learning and development.

Our EYFS Vision

We believe that early childhood is a unique and valuable stage of life in its own right. Our EYFS provision creates a nurturing environment where children feel safe, secure and happy to explore and learn.

Learning Through Play

In EYFS, children learn through play. We provide a rich learning environment with carefully planned activities that cover all areas of learning. Children have opportunities for both adult-led activities and child-initiated play.

Areas of Learning

The EYFS Framework identifies seven areas of learning:

Prime Areas

Communication & Language

Listening, attention, understanding and speaking

Physical Development

Gross and fine motor skills, health and self-care

Personal, Social & Emotional

Self-confidence, managing feelings, relationships

Specific Areas

Literacy

Reading and writing foundations

Mathematics

Numbers and numerical patterns

Understanding the World

People, culture, communities and technology

Expressive Arts & Design

Creating, exploring and using media

Our EYFS Environment

Our EYFS classrooms provide:

  • Continuous provision with learning areas indoors and outdoors
  • A balance of adult-led and child-initiated learning
  • Resources that promote curiosity and exploration
  • A safe, nurturing environment where children feel secure
  • Access to a dedicated outdoor learning area

Phonics & Early Reading

We follow a systematic synthetic phonics programme to teach children to read. Daily phonics sessions develop children's ability to read and write. Children also enjoy regular story times and develop a love of books.

Assessment

In EYFS, we assess children through ongoing observation. At the end of Reception, children are assessed against the Early Learning Goals. We share progress with parents through:

  • Parents' evenings
  • Learning journals
  • End of year reports
  • Informal conversations at drop-off and pick-up
