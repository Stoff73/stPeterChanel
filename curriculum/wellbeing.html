Wellbeing

Home/Curriculum/Wellbeing

We believe that children's mental health and wellbeing are just as important as their physical health. A happy child is a child who can learn.

Our Approach

At St Peter Chanel, we take a whole-school approach to mental health and wellbeing. We aim to:

  • Create a safe, nurturing environment where children feel valued
  • Teach children to understand and manage their emotions
  • Build resilience and positive coping strategies
  • Identify and support children who may be struggling
  • Work with families to support children's wellbeing

What We Do

PSHE Curriculum

Weekly lessons on mental health, emotions and relationships.

Zones of Regulation

Teaching children to recognise and regulate their emotions.

Mindfulness

Quiet reflection and calming activities built into the day.

Pastoral Support

A team of trained staff available to support children.

Mental Health Team

We have a dedicated team to support children's mental health:

  • Mental Health Lead: Oversees whole-school approach
  • Learning Mentors: Support individual children
  • SENCo: Supports children with additional needs
  • Class Teachers: First point of contact for parents

Support Available

We offer a range of support for children who need extra help:

  • 1:1 support with learning mentors
  • Small group interventions
  • Nurture groups
  • Social skills groups
  • Referrals to external services (CAMHS, counselling)

For Parents

If you are concerned about your child's mental health or wellbeing, please speak to their class teacher in the first instance. We are here to help and can work together to support your child.
