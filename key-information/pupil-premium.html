Pupil Premium

Home/Key Information/Pupil Premium

The Pupil Premium is additional funding given to schools to help disadvantaged pupils achieve their full potential.

What is Pupil Premium?

The Pupil Premium is additional funding provided to schools to support pupils who:

  • Are currently eligible for Free School Meals (FSM)
  • Have been eligible for FSM at any point in the last 6 years (Ever 6)
  • Are looked after children (LAC) or previously looked after
  • Have parents in the armed forces (Service Premium)

How We Use Our Funding

At St Peter Chanel, we use our Pupil Premium funding to:

  • Provide additional teaching and learning support
  • Deliver targeted interventions
  • Support social, emotional and mental health needs
  • Fund enrichment activities and trips
  • Provide access to breakfast club and after-school activities
  • Purchase resources and equipment

Our Strategy

Our Pupil Premium strategy focuses on:

  • Quality First Teaching - Ensuring all pupils receive excellent teaching
  • Targeted Academic Support - Small group and 1:1 interventions
  • Wider Strategies - Addressing barriers beyond the classroom

Impact

We monitor the impact of our Pupil Premium spending through:

  • Tracking pupil progress and attainment data
  • Analysing the gap between disadvantaged and other pupils
  • Regular review meetings
  • Governor oversight

Free School Meals

If you think your child may be eligible for Free School Meals, we encourage you to apply. Even if your child is in Reception to Year 2 and receives Universal Infant Free School Meals, registering for FSM ensures the school receives Pupil Premium funding to support your child.
