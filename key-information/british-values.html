British Values

Home/Key Information/British Values

At St Peter Chanel, we actively promote the fundamental British values of democracy, the rule of law, individual liberty, and mutual respect and tolerance.

Our Approach

These values are woven throughout our curriculum and school life, and align closely with our Catholic values and mission to "Inspire, Challenge & Empower."

The Five British Values

Democracy

Children learn about democracy through:

  • School Council elections
  • Class votes on activities and topics
  • Learning about Parliament and government
  • Pupil voice opportunities

The Rule of Law

Children understand the importance of rules through:

  • School rules and behaviour expectations
  • Understanding consequences
  • Learning about laws and why they exist
  • Visits from police and community services

Individual Liberty

Children develop a sense of personal freedom through:

  • Making choices about learning
  • E-safety and online choices
  • Understanding rights and responsibilities
  • Developing self-esteem and confidence

Mutual Respect

Children learn to respect others through:

  • Our Catholic values of love and respect
  • PSHE lessons
  • Anti-bullying work
  • Collaborative learning

Tolerance of Different Faiths and Beliefs

Children develop tolerance through:

  • RE lessons covering world religions
  • Celebrating diversity in our community
  • Assemblies and discussions
  • Our welcome of "all faiths and no faiths"

How We Promote British Values

  • Through our curriculum, especially PSHE, RE and History
  • In assemblies and collective worship
  • Through School Council and pupil leadership
  • By modelling positive relationships
  • Through extra-curricular activities and community events
