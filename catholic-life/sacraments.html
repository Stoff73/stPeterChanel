Sacraments

Home/Catholic Life/Sacraments

The Sacraments are sacred signs through which we receive God's grace. We prepare children for the Sacraments of Reconciliation and First Holy Communion in Year 3.

Sacramental Preparation

In Year 3, children who have been baptised as Catholics are prepared to receive the Sacraments of Reconciliation (Confession) and First Holy Communion (Eucharist). This is done in partnership with parents and the parish.

First Reconciliation

The Sacrament of Reconciliation (Confession) allows us to receive God's forgiveness for our sins. Children make their First Confession during Lent, before receiving First Holy Communion.

First Holy Communion

In the Eucharist, we receive the Body and Blood of Christ. First Holy Communion is celebrated in the summer term, usually in May or June.

The Programme

Preparation for the Sacraments includes:

  • Religious Education lessons in school
  • Parent meetings and workshops
  • Retreat days
  • Practice sessions
  • The celebrations themselves

Parent Involvement

Parents are the primary educators of their children in faith. During sacramental preparation, parents are asked to:

  • Attend parent meetings
  • Support their child's preparation at home
  • Attend Sunday Mass regularly with their child
  • Participate in the celebrations

Requirements

To receive the Sacraments at St Peter Chanel, children must:

  • Be baptised Catholic (a copy of the baptism certificate is required)
  • Be in Year 3 or above
  • Have parents committed to supporting their preparation
  • Attend Sunday Mass regularly

Other Sacraments

If your child has not been baptised but you would like them to be, please contact the parish. The Sacrament of Confirmation is usually received in secondary school.

Questions?

If you have questions about sacramental preparation, please speak to your child's class teacher or contact the school office.
