Mini Vinnies

Home/Catholic Life/Mini Vinnies

Mini Vinnies is the primary school section of the St Vincent de Paul Society. Our young members put their faith into action by helping others.

What is Mini Vinnies?

Mini Vinnies is a group of young people who want to make a difference. Inspired by the Gospel and the example of St Vincent de Paul, we learn about social justice and take action to help those in need.

Mini Vinnies is part of the SVP (St Vincent de Paul Society), one of the largest voluntary organisations in the world dedicated to tackling poverty and disadvantage.

What We Do

Helping Others

We organise collections for food banks, care homes and those in need.

Fundraising

We raise money for charitable causes through events and activities.

Prayer

We pray for those in need and reflect on how we can help.

Community

We build friendships and work together to make a difference.

Our Projects

Throughout the year, Mini Vinnies get involved in various projects:

  • Harvest Appeal: Collecting food for local food banks
  • Christmas Hampers: Creating hampers for families in need
  • Lenten Giving: Supporting CAFOD and other charities
  • Care Home Visits: Visiting and performing for elderly residents
  • Caring for Creation: Environmental projects
  • Acts of Kindness: Random acts of kindness around school

Join Us

Mini Vinnies is open to children in Key Stage 2 who want to put their faith into action. We meet at Friday lunchtime to plan activities and reflect on our work.

If you would like to join, speak to your class teacher or come along to one of our meetings!

Our Mini Vinnies Promise

"I promise to be a good Mini Vinnie,
to love and respect all people,
to be the hands and heart of Jesus,
to bring joy to those in need."

St Vincent de Paul

St Vincent de Paul (1581-1660) dedicated his life to serving the poor. He founded charitable organisations and inspired others to help those in need. He is the patron saint of charitable works.
