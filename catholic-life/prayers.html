Prayers

Home/Catholic Life/Prayers

Prayer is at the heart of our school day. We come together to pray, giving thanks to God and asking for His guidance.

Daily Prayers

We pray together at the start and end of each day, before and after lunch, and at key moments throughout the day.

Our Father

Our Father, who art in heaven,
hallowed be Thy name.
Thy Kingdom come,
Thy will be done,
on earth as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread,
and forgive us our trespasses,
as we forgive those who trespass against us.
And lead us not into temptation,
but deliver us from evil.
Amen.

Hail Mary

Hail Mary, full of grace,
the Lord is with thee.
Blessed art thou among women,
and blessed is the fruit of thy womb, Jesus.
Holy Mary, Mother of God,
pray for us sinners,
now and at the hour of our death.
Amen.

Glory Be

Glory be to the Father,
and to the Son,
and to the Holy Spirit.
As it was in the beginning,
is now, and ever shall be,
world without end.
Amen.

Morning Prayer

Father in heaven, you love me,
you're with me night and day.
I want to love you always
in all I do and say.
I'll try to please you, Father.
Bless me through the day.
Amen.

Grace Before Meals

Bless us, O Lord,
and these Thy gifts,
which we are about to receive
from Thy bounty,
through Christ Our Lord.
Amen.

End of Day Prayer

God our Father, I come to say
thank you for your love today.
Thank you for my family
and all the friends you give to me.
Guard me in the dark of night
and in the morning send your light.
Amen.

The Sign of the Cross

We begin and end our prayers with the Sign of the Cross:

In the name of the Father, and of the Son, and of the Holy Spirit. Amen.

Prayer in Our School

  • Every classroom has a prayer corner
  • We pray together at assemblies
  • Classes celebrate liturgies throughout the year
  • We pray the Angelus at midday
  • During May and October, we pray the Rosary

Praying at Home

We encourage families to pray together at home. Prayer doesn't have to be formal - you can talk to God in your own words, share your worries and give thanks for blessings.
