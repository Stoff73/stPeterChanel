Outdoor Learning

Home/Children/Outdoor Learning

We believe learning should extend beyond the classroom. Our outdoor learning programme helps children connect with nature and develop important skills.

Learning Outside

At St Peter Chanel, we recognise the value of learning outdoors. Our outdoor learning programme provides children with opportunities to explore, investigate and learn in the natural environment.

Research shows that outdoor learning improves children's physical health, mental wellbeing, social skills and academic achievement. We aim to give every child regular opportunities to learn outside.

Our Outdoor Spaces

School Garden

Our school garden is used for growing vegetables and flowers, learning about plants and wildlife, and peaceful reflection.

Outdoor Classroom

A dedicated space for outdoor lessons, complete with seating and a canopy for all-weather learning.

Nature Area

A wild area where children can explore nature, observe wildlife and develop a love for the environment.

What We Do

Forest School

Regular Forest School sessions where children develop practical skills, confidence and a connection with nature.

Gardening

Growing fruit, vegetables and flowers in our school garden, learning about plant life cycles and healthy eating.

Science

Outdoor investigations and experiments, studying habitats, weather, and the natural world.

Art & Creativity

Using natural materials for art projects, observational drawing, and creative expression.

Outdoor Reading

Quiet reading sessions in the garden, using nature as inspiration for creative writing.

Reflection

Peaceful outdoor spaces for prayer, meditation and spiritual development.

Benefits of Outdoor Learning

  • Physical health: Fresh air, exercise and connection with nature
  • Mental wellbeing: Reduced stress, improved mood and resilience
  • Social skills: Teamwork, communication and collaboration
  • Academic achievement: Hands-on learning improves understanding and retention
  • Environmental awareness: Developing respect for nature and sustainability
  • Confidence: Taking risks in a safe environment builds self-esteem

Eco Committee

Our Eco Committee is a group of dedicated pupils who lead on environmental initiatives in school. They work alongside the School Council to promote sustainability and care for God's creation.

Activities include:

  • Recycling and waste reduction initiatives
  • Energy saving campaigns
  • Litter picks and caring for our grounds
  • Wildlife projects and bird feeding
  • Working towards Eco-Schools awards
