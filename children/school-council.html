Our School Council gives pupils a voice in the running of our school and helps develop leadership skills.

What is School Council?

The School Council is a group of elected pupils who represent the views of their classmates. Council members meet regularly to discuss ideas for improving our school and making it an even better place to learn.

Each class from Year 1 to Year 6 elects two representatives at the start of the school year. These councillors are the voice of their class and bring ideas and suggestions to council meetings.

What We Do

  • Meet fortnightly to discuss school matters
  • Collect ideas from classmates using suggestion boxes
  • Present pupil views to the Headteacher and staff
  • Help organise charity events and fundraising
  • Welcome visitors to our school
  • Help with school assemblies and events
  • Work on projects to improve our school

Recent Achievements

  • Organised Children in Need fundraising activities
  • Helped design new playground markings
  • Led on reducing plastic waste in school
  • Improved the book corner in the library
  • Created a buddy system for new pupils

How to Get Involved

At the start of each school year, all pupils in Years 1-6 have the opportunity to stand for election. Candidates give a speech to their class explaining why they would make a good councillor, and then their classmates vote.

Even if you're not a councillor, you can still get involved by:

  • Sharing your ideas with your class councillors
  • Writing suggestions for the suggestion box
  • Supporting School Council initiatives
